First-In-First-Out Algorithm (FIFO)
FIFO operates on the principle of evicting the oldest page in memory. It maintains a queue of pages and replaces the page that entered the memory earliest.

Second Chance Algorithm (SC)
Second Chance, a variation of FIFO, includes a reference bit to give pages a "second chance" before eviction. If a page is referenced again before eviction, it's moved to the end of the queue.

Least Recently Used Algorithm (LRU)
LRU replaces the least recently used page. It relies on the principle that pages used in the recent past are less likely to be used soon, making them candidates for replacement.

OPT, an idealistic algorithm, removes the page that won't be used for the longest duration in the future. It assumes perfect knowledge of future references, making it a benchmark for comparison.
